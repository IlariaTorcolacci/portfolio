OVERVIEW

During the 'Virtual and Physical Prototyping' course at @PoliMI, my team and I created an olfactive display system prototype.

This system, enhanced with an AR experience developed in Unity using the @MetaQuest3, aims to deeply immerse users in the role of a perfumer. It provides them with comprehensive knowledge of fragrances, enabling them to experiment and test various perfume mixtures.

AR-Enhanced Fragrance Experience

This project defined user interactions for an educational AR experience, complementing an olfactory display with rich visual and informative content. You selected six fragrances, preparing corresponding 3D models and images for import into Unity via Vuforia. Crucially, you then scaled, positioned, animated, and colored these 3D models while setting up printed image targets for AR recognition. Objects, canvases, and buttons were precisely positioned on these Image Targets, with a custom script ensuring full functionality. Finally, the entire Unity project was exported to Android, featuring fully opaque graphics for optimal visibility and clear information delivery.

TECHNICAL APPROACH

The project began with a conceptual prototype for an AR-controlled olfactory display using six piezo atomizers. The initial design featured a hexagonal atomizer arrangement in a spherical casing, controlled by an AR board with capacitive touch sensors, but faced challenges with 3D-printed components absorbing liquid and scent. Subsequent iterations focused on improving refillability, assembly, and user interaction, leading to a semi-spherical chassis and redesigned nozzle to accommodate glass vials and correct the AR board's orientation. The final design optimized maintenance, transportation, and aesthetics by fixing atomizers in a secure casing, hiding screws, and consolidating cabling for easier dismantling and a streamlined user experience.

TAKEAWAYS

Proper AR experience come give you a deep dive in some worlds

Developing the AR-enhanced Olfactory Display System was a complex, yet successful endeavor. While integrating AR technology to improve the perfume testing experience met initial requirements, significant challenges emerged during the iterative process. The prototype, though robust and portable, faces disassembly difficulties due to numerous cables and the need to open the chassis for disconnection, a complexity also seen in the nozzle assembly. A major hurdle was the lack of communication between the physical AR control board and the mobile device, necessitating mirrored interactions in the AR environment. Despite these integration challenges, the system successfully fires six scents, utilizes new spill-proof 2ml medical cartridges, and features functional capacitive touch sensors with distinct states. AR functionalities effectively display scent information and ingredient animations when users scan labels, delivering a visually enriched and informative experience, with future plans to integrate physical and virtual interactions via Bluetooth.
